Graphic Design & House Wiring • Weeks 17–18

Graphic Design
Weeks 17–18 • Capstone Phases 2–3
▶
Week 17
Production Plan • Workflow Sequence • Asset Prep
▶
Monday ▶

GD Week 17 — Monday

WAL
Students finalize their Phase 2 Production Plan structure and confirm deliverables for their Capstone.
Instruction
Model converting Phase 1 concept info into Phase 2: deliverables list, elements list, and workflow sequence.
Practice
Students draft or revise Deliverables, Visible Elements, and Asset List for their project.
Success Criteria
Production Plan shows complete deliverables, a clear asset list, and early workflow steps for each deliverable.
Teacher Moves
1:1 checks, verify project scope, redirect oversized ideas, approve or adjust deliverables list.
Tuesday ▶

GD Week 17 — Tuesday

WAL
Students refine workflow sequence steps and confirm required assets for each deliverable.
Instruction
Demonstrate breaking production into small, trackable steps (5–12 steps per deliverable) with realistic timing.
Practice
Update workflow sequence and annotate which assets belong to which deliverables and steps.
Success Criteria
Workflow is ordered, realistic, and aligned with both deliverables and asset requirements.
Teacher Moves
Check step clarity, flag missing steps, enforce sequencing from roughs to finals, approve sequences.
Wednesday ▶

GD Week 17 — Wednesday

WAL
Students complete all Production Plan sections before Thursday’s independent production work.
Instruction
Review sample strong Production Plans and walk through a checklist of required sections for completion.
Practice
Students finalize all four Phase 2 sections and submit for approval.
Success Criteria
Phase 2 Production Plan is complete, coherent, and approved for Phase 3 asset production.
Teacher Moves
Final checks, document approvals, redirect incomplete plans, schedule make-up time for missing work.
Thursday ▶

GD Week 17 — Thursday

WAL
Students begin Phase 3: Asset Production using their approved Production Plans.
Instruction
Demonstrate asset folder organization, file naming conventions, and version control for graphics and photos.
Practice
Create Asset Folder and begin first assets (photos, drawings, vector icons, type samples).
Success Criteria
Asset folder exists and includes at least 2–3 completed or in-progress assets clearly labeled.
Teacher Moves
Confirm folder structure, check file naming, approve first assets, redirect low-quality or off-concept work.
Friday ▶

GD Week 17 — Friday

WAL
Students continue asset creation and begin early assembly steps in layout software.
Instruction
Mini-demo: exporting assets for layout and placing them into InDesign/Photoshop with correct resolution.
Practice
Work session: finish several key assets and start assembling the first full layout draft.
Success Criteria
5–8 assets complete and at least one main deliverable has a visible first layout draft started.
Teacher Moves
Check asset quality, verify export settings, approve students to move deeper into assembly.
Week 18
Assembly • Refinement • Export • Presentations
▶
Monday ▶

GD Week 18 — Monday

WAL
Students assemble final compositions using their completed assets and layout plans.
Instruction
Demo: layout refinement, alignment, grids, visual hierarchy, and color matching to the original concept.
Practice
Students build a first complete draft of all required deliverables based on their Production Plan.
Success Criteria
All assets placed in layout; draft is complete enough for detailed critique and refinement.
Teacher Moves
Approve drafts for refinement, correct hierarchy issues, reinforce grid and alignment use.
Tuesday ▶

GD Week 18 — Tuesday

WAL
Students refine drafts based on feedback and polish typography and spacing.
Instruction
Mini-demo on kerning, tracking, leading, and color harmonization across all deliverables.
Practice
Students revise typesetting, adjust margins and bleed settings, and refine composition details.
Success Criteria
Typographic consistency, improved clarity, and visibly more polished layouts compared to Monday.
Teacher Moves
Provide targeted micro-adjustments, enforce consistency, flag lingering alignment/spacing issues.
Wednesday ▶

GD Week 18 — Wednesday

WAL
Students finalize export settings for print and digital versions of the project.
Instruction
Demo: exporting PDFs, RGB vs CMYK, bleed and slug, embedding fonts, and file compression.
Practice
Students export all deliverables according to the rubric and submit files for verification.
Success Criteria
Files have correct format, color mode, resolution, and naming conventions for turn-in.
Teacher Moves
Approve exports, fix color and bleed issues, guide students to correct naming or missing files.
Thursday ▶

GD Week 18 — Thursday

WAL
Students assemble and rehearse their Capstone presentations.
Instruction
Demo: presentation structure (concept → process → final) and design vocabulary for explaining choices.
Practice
Build slides or boards showing before/after, key assets, and final designs; rehearse short presentations.
Success Criteria
Slides clearly communicate purpose, audience, process steps, and final results.
Teacher Moves
Check clarity, adjust slide order, reinforce use of design terminology.
Friday ▶

GD Week 18 — Friday

WAL
Students present their Capstone projects and reflect on their design process.
Instruction
Teacher models presentation expectations and how to respond to questions about design decisions.
Practice
Students deliver their presentations and respond to brief peer/teacher questions.
Success Criteria
Professional, clear presentations with complete finalized deliverables and a coherent process story.
Teacher Moves
Evaluate using rubric, document evidence for evaluation, provide final feedback.
House Wiring
Weeks 17–18 • Walls, circuits, diagrams, testing
▶
Week 17
Device installation • Wiring checks • Safety procedures
▶
Monday ▶

HW Week 17 — Monday

WAL
Students identify wiring errors and correct routing from previous work on the half-wall.
Instruction
Model common wiring mistakes and basic residential code standards (staples, bends, stripping, box fill).
Practice
Students inspect their wall wiring, fix incorrect runs, and prepare boxes for devices.
Success Criteria
All cables correctly routed, stapled, and entering boxes appropriately; wall is ready for device installation.
Teacher Moves
Check staple spacing, strain relief, and routing; approve corrections or send students back to fix issues.
Tuesday ▶

HW Week 17 — Tuesday

WAL
Students install outlets and switches following correct conductor placement and device orientation.
Instruction
Demo: strip lengths, clockwise wire hooks, hot vs neutral placement, and basic switch loop wiring.
Practice
Students install outlet(s) and a single-pole or 3-way switch depending on their wall design.
Success Criteria
Devices are mounted flush, screws tightened, correct conductors on hot/neutral/ground terminals.
Teacher Moves
Inspect each device, fix reversed hots, check ground bonding, and correct loose connections.
Wednesday ▶

HW Week 17 — Wednesday

WAL
Students test continuity and identify faults in their wired circuits.
Instruction
Demo: multimeter continuity checks, open hot/neutral troubleshooting, and safe test procedures.
Practice
Students test each device and run, mark problem spots, and correct miswired connections.
Success Criteria
All circuits pass continuity checks with no open connections at devices or junction points.
Teacher Moves
Validate continuity procedure, assist with tracing faults, model systematic troubleshooting.
Thursday ▶

HW Week 17 — Thursday

WAL
Students complete final wiring corrections and prepare walls for cover plate installation.
Instruction
Review proper device mounting depth, box fill, and screw torque; reinforce neat conductor organization.
Practice
Students re-check all devices, correct any flagged issues, and organize conductors neatly in boxes.
Success Criteria
All devices correctly wired and seated, no exposed copper beyond terminals, neat interior organization.
Teacher Moves
Quality-control check of each wall before plates; document any remaining issues for Friday.
Friday ▶

HW Week 17 — Friday

WAL
Students mount cover plates and perform a final visual inspection of all devices and wiring.
Instruction
Demo: leveling plates, screw orientation, and final appearance standards for finished walls.
Practice
Install all cover plates, perform a final peer-check, and prepare for Week 18 live testing.
Success Criteria
Walls present clean, consistent appearance and are ready for energizing and testing in Week 18.
Teacher Moves
Conduct final visual walkthrough, record completion status for each student.
Week 18
Final testing • Diagrams • Capstone documentation
▶
Monday ▶

HW Week 18 — Monday

WAL
Students prepare for safe live testing of their circuits.
Instruction
Model GFCI reset, line/load identification, and step-by-step safe energizing procedures.
Practice
Students verify that devices are tight, covers secured, and circuits are ready for testing.
Success Criteria
Walls meet safety checklist requirements and are approved for testing.
Teacher Moves
Perform safety checks, sign off walls for testing or require corrections before power.
Tuesday ▶

HW Week 18 — Tuesday

WAL
Students conduct full circuit testing and interpret tester results.
Instruction
Demo: plug-in tester codes (hot/neutral reverse, open ground), resetting breakers, and tracing faults.
Practice
Students test each outlet and switch circuit, logging results in a testing sheet.
Success Criteria
All circuits test correctly with no fault codes; any issues are identified for repair.
Teacher Moves
Support troubleshooting, show structured approach to isolating wiring errors.
Wednesday ▶

HW Week 18 — Wednesday

WAL
Students create a final wiring diagram of their wall, using correct symbols and paths.
Instruction
Demo: drawing the circuit accurately on paper or digitally, using symbols, labels, and clear routing.
Practice
Students diagram their entire wall, including power source, switches, outlets, and device connections.
Success Criteria
Diagram accurately reflects the physical wall, including all devices and conductors.
Teacher Moves
Check diagrams for accuracy, require corrections when wiring and drawing do not match.
Thursday ▶

HW Week 18 — Thursday

WAL
Students complete all capstone documentation for their wall project.
Instruction
Review expectations for labeling, legends, and written notes that accompany the diagram.
Practice
Students finalize diagrams, add legends and labels, and complete any written explanations.
Success Criteria
Capstone documentation (diagram + legend + notes) is complete, legible, and aligned with wiring.
Teacher Moves
Verify completeness, flag missing labeling, and confirm readiness for final submission.
Friday ▶

HW Week 18 — Friday

WAL
Students submit their capstone and complete a brief reflection on their wiring work.
Instruction
Outline final turn-in requirements and reflection prompts (challenges, skills learned, safety habits).
Practice
Students submit diagrams and written reflection; optional quick share or gallery walk.
Success Criteria
Capstone wiring project and documentation are fully submitted with a thoughtful reflection.
Teacher Moves
Collect work, record scores, and document evidence for evaluation.
